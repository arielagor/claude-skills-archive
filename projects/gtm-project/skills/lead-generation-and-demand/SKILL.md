# Lead Generation & Demand

## When this fires

Building lead lists, demand-gen campaigns, lead magnets, lead scoring, or multi-channel acquisition strategy for any property in `PORTFOLIO_PROPERTIES.md`. Most upstream framing assumes a venture-funded B2B SaaS company with a $30k to $50k a month paid-channel budget; Ariel's properties run at a different scale and mix (a $67 to $399 template store, a $15K to $250K consulting practice, App Store IAP apps, a tools directory). Use the strategy frameworks below qualitatively, not the literal dollar figures.

## Workspace context to read first

- `briefs/<property>.md` for the property this demand-gen work targets (most often `briefs/agor-consulting.md` for B2B outbound; check the matching brief for `modelstack`, `scored-tools`, or `ios-apps` for others).
- `about/me.md` for voice on any lead-magnet copy, nurture email, or landing-page copy drafted here.
- `strategy/brand.md` for cross-property positioning.

## Two different jobs, two different assets

Upstream's playbook conflates "how do I decide who to target and how to nurture them" with "how do I get an actual list of real companies and contacts." Split those:

- **Strategy and design** (ICP definition, funnel mapping, lead magnet formats, gating rules, lead scoring, attribution, CAC framing) stays here, backed by `references/upstream-playbook.md`. Use it to decide what a good lead looks like and what to offer them, not to fabricate a channel budget or a specific lead count for a property this size.
- **Actually sourcing a list of real prospects** hands off to `~/.claude/skills/library-business-prospect-list/SKILL.md`. That skill covers the mechanics upstream's "Lead Research Framework" only gestures at: the free Data Axle Reference Solutions access via a public library card, the Census batch geocoder plus Haversine distance-sort pipeline, owner and decision-maker title filtering, institutional-NAICS exclusion, and the xlsx plus portrait-PDF output. Use it whenever the ask is geographic or local B2B (a territory list, "businesses near X," door-knock or cold-call targets) rather than a pure inbound content funnel.

## Dedup and enrichment against what Ariel already has

Before treating any sourced or list-built lead as new, check GBrain:

- `mcp__gbrain__query` or `search` by company name and contact name to see whether the prospect (or their company) already exists among the 5,400+ people and 368 companies tracked there.
- `mcp__gbrain__get_backlinks` on any match to see whether a relationship already exists (an email thread, a text, a past meeting). A match means this is a warm re-engagement, not a fresh lead, and the right next skill is `networking-outreach` (which itself hands off to `precall-brief` once a meeting is booked), not a cold demand-gen sequence.
- Only genuinely new contacts get scored and nurtured as new leads per the scoring model in `references/upstream-playbook.md`. New contacts who survive outreach should get filed into GBrain (`put_page`, `add_tag`, `add_link`) so the next lead-gen pass doesn't re-source them.

Deal-stage and pipeline tracking for the consulting line lives in the file-based revenue scorecard (`~/.claude/revenue-scorecard/`), not a CRM SaaS, per `data/REMAP.md` Row 1. Nothing generated by this skill should assume a HubSpot- or Salesforce-style pipeline object exists.

## Reference material

`references/upstream-playbook.md` holds the full upstream playbook: the ICP framework (firmographics, psychographics, technographics), the TOFU/MOFU/BOFU funnel map, the channel strategy matrix and budget-allocation table (illustrative B2B SaaS figures, not Ariel's actual spend), lead magnet formats and hooks, the landing page framework, the lead scoring model, email nurture sequence timing, CAC benchmarks, attribution modeling, the UTM structure, account-based sales criteria, and the do/don't and error-handling tables.

## Dry-run rule

Lead lists, lead-magnet drafts, landing-page copy, and nurture-sequence email copy are all artifacts: save them to `content/<platform>/drafts/YYYY-MM-DD_topic-slug.md` or the relevant project location. No list gets called, emailed, or texted, and no nurture sequence gets bulk-sent, without Ariel's explicit, in-the-moment go. There is no bulk-send platform in this stack (`data/REMAP.md` Row 3), so any "send this to N leads" ask gets drafted individually via the Gmail MCP (`mcp__claude_ai_Gmail__create_draft`) or flagged as a manual, future distribution decision, not executed.
